## Problem Statement
Scam ads are a systemic problem on major social media platforms (Meta, YouTube, TikTok, X). These fraudulent advertisements use:
- AI deepfake celebrity endorsements
- Fake investment platforms
- Fake bank and government alerts
- Impersonated financial influencers
- Phishing schemes

## How Scam Ads Work

### The Three-Step Process
1. **Scammers**: Create fake ads using stolen identities, deepfakes, and automation
2. **Platforms**: Distribute ads to millions and profit from every view
3. **Users**: Trust the platform, lose money, data, and privacy

### Cost Efficiency
- **$8 USD** in ad spend reaches **1,000 users** on YouTube
- Even **0.1% victimization rate** can destroy families financially
- Highest ROI crime industry globally

## Why Platforms Allow This

1. **Revenue Priority**: Scam ads pay high CPC/CPM rates
2. **High Review Costs**: Requires OCR, frame analysis, deepfake detection
3. **Low Incentive**: Not treated as systemic risk
4. **Profit Model**: Scam ads increase global CPC for all advertisers
